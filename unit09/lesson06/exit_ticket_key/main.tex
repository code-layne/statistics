\textbf{Directions:} For each scenario, (1) name the correct inference procedure, and
(2) justify your choice in one sentence.

\begin{notesbox}{Scenario 1}
A marine biologist records the water temperature ($^\circ$C, $x$) and the number of dolphin
sightings per day ($y$) at 30 randomly selected observation sites. She wants to test whether
water temperature is linearly related to dolphin sightings.

\vspace{0.1in}
\textbf{Name the procedure:} \ans{$t$-test for slope}

\vspace{0.1in}
\textbf{Justify} (one sentence):
\ans{Both variables are quantitative and the researcher wants to test whether the true slope of the population regression line ($\beta$) equals zero, which calls for a $t$-test for slope.}
\end{notesbox}

\begin{notesbox}{Scenario 2}
A market researcher randomly samples 200 consumers and records which of 4 brands they
prefer (Brand A, B, C, or D). The researcher wants to test whether consumers prefer the
four brands equally.

\vspace{0.1in}
\textbf{Name the procedure:} \ans{Chi-square goodness of fit test}

\vspace{0.1in}
\textbf{Justify} (one sentence):
\ans{Brand preference is a single categorical variable with 4 categories, and the researcher is testing whether the variable follows a specified distribution (equal proportions), which calls for a chi-square GOF test.}
\end{notesbox}
