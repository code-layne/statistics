For a random sample of 22 students, a researcher regresses exam score ($y$) on hours
studied ($x$). The hypotheses are H$_0$: $\beta = 0$ vs.\ H$_a$: $\beta \ne 0$.
The regression output gives $b = 3.6$ and $SE_b = 0.90$.

\begin{enumerate}

  \item Calculate the $t$-test statistic. Show your work.

        \vspace{0.05in}
        $t = \dfrac{b}{SE_b} = \dfrac{\blank{2cm}}{\blank{2cm}} = \blank{2cm}$

        \vspace{0.15in}

  \item What are the degrees of freedom for this test?

        \vspace{0.05in}
        $df = n - 2 = \blank{2cm} - 2 = \blank{2cm}$

        \vspace{0.15in}

  \item The $p$-value from technology is 0.003. Is this greater than or less than
        $\alpha = 0.05$? Based on this, should you reject or fail to reject H$_0$?
        Explain briefly.

        \vspace{0.05in}
        \writelines{3}

\end{enumerate}
