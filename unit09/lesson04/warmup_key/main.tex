A 95\% confidence interval for the slope $\beta$ relating study hours ($x$) to
exam score ($y$) for students at one school was computed as $(0.03,\ 0.21)$ points
per additional hour of study.

\begin{enumerate}

  \item Does this confidence interval provide evidence that $\beta \ne 0$? Justify.

        \ans{Yes. Since 0 is not in the interval $(0.03,\ 0.21)$, the CI provides
        evidence that $\beta \ne 0$; there is a positive linear relationship between
        study hours and exam score.}

  \item What does $\beta = 0$ mean in context?

        \ans{$\beta = 0$ means that in the population, studying more hours has no
        linear effect on exam score --- there is no real linear relationship between
        study time and score.}

  \item A researcher now wants to formally \textbf{test} whether $\beta = 0$ using a
        significance test rather than a confidence interval.
        What would H$_0$ and H$_a$ be?

        H$_0$: \ans{$\beta = 0$ (no linear relationship between study hours and exam score)}

        \vspace{0.05in}

        H$_a$: \ans{$\beta \ne 0$ (there is a linear relationship between study hours and exam score)}

\end{enumerate}
