\begin{learningtargetbox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=5pt]
  \item I can explain why the sample slope $b$ of an LSRL is a statistic that
        varies from sample to sample.
  \item I can state the central question of Unit 9: could the observed sample
        slope $b$ have arisen by chance even if the true population slope
        $\beta = 0$?
  \item I can interpret the slope and $y$-intercept of an LSRL in context and
        explain why a non-zero sample slope alone does not prove a real linear
        relationship in the population.
\end{enumerate}
\end{learningtargetbox}
