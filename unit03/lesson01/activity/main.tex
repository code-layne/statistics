\vspace{0.1in}

\begin{scenariobox}[Context]{sky}
\small
Your group will evaluate four data collection scenarios and decide whether the data
collected can be trusted to represent the target population. For each, circle
\textbf{Trustworthy} or \textbf{Not Trustworthy}, identify whose voices are
\emph{missing}, and describe how the conclusions might be distorted.
\end{scenariobox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Scenario A:} A local TV station wants to know whether residents support a new
sports stadium. They invite viewers to call a hotline to vote YES or NO.
After one hour, 87\% of 340 callers say YES.

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item Trustworthy \quad / \quad Not Trustworthy --- Circle one.
  \item Who is likely calling? \blank{8cm}
  \item Whose opinions are missing? \blank{8cm}
  \item Does the 87\% accurately represent the community's view? Explain.\\
        \writeline\\[1pt]\writeline
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Scenario B:} A school wants to know whether students prefer longer lunch
periods. The principal randomly selects 40 students from the school roster using
a random number table and interviews each one.

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item Trustworthy \quad / \quad Not Trustworthy --- Circle one.
  \item How were students selected? \blank{8cm}
  \item Is the sample likely to represent all students? Why or why not?\\
        \writeline\\[1pt]\writeline
  \item Could you trust a conclusion like ``70\% of students want longer lunches''?
        \blank{5cm}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Scenario C:} A pharmaceutical company wants to know if doctors recommend their
drug. They survey doctors who attended their company-sponsored conference.

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item Trustworthy \quad / \quad Not Trustworthy --- Circle one.
  \item What type of doctors are in this sample? \blank{8cm}
  \item What type of doctors are missing? \blank{8cm}
  \item How would this distort the company's estimate? \blank{8cm}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Scenario D:} A researcher is studying average household income in a city.
She mails a survey to every 50th address in the city's postal database and
gets responses from 200 households.

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item Is the selection method chance-based? \blank{5cm}
  \item Is this sample trustworthy? What concerns, if any, remain?\\
        \writeline\\[1pt]\writeline
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.12in}

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\textbf{Group Synthesis:} Based on your four scenarios, complete this rule in your
own words:

\vspace{6pt}
``A data collection method produces trustworthy conclusions when \ldots''\\[6pt]
\writeline\\[1pt]\writeline\\[1pt]\writeline
\end{practicebox}
