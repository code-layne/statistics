\begin{scenariobox}[Context]{sky}
\small
The principal wants to know how students feel about the new lunch menu. She asks
the first 30 students who enter the cafeteria on Monday. 24 of them say they like it.
\end{scenariobox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item \ans{Non-chance — the principal chose students based on who arrived first (a convenience sample).}
  \item \ans{Students who arrive early to the cafeteria (likely hungry or enthusiastic about food).}
  \item \ans{Students who arrive late, bring their own lunch, or eat elsewhere; students with dietary restrictions who may dislike the menu options.}
  \item \ans{No. The 80\% cannot be trusted as representative of all students because the sample was not randomly selected. Only students who arrived early were included, which may systematically favor students who like the cafeteria. This is a non-chance (convenience) sample, so the conclusions are untrustworthy.}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}
