\textbf{Scenario A:} TV station call-in poll.
\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=6pt]
  \item \ans{Not Trustworthy}
  \item \ans{Mostly strong supporters of the stadium (motivated to call).}
  \item \ans{Residents who are neutral or mildly opposed, and those who do not watch that station.}
  \item \ans{No. Only highly motivated viewers called, so the 87\% dramatically over-represents enthusiastic supporters (voluntary response bias).}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.08in}

\textbf{Scenario B:} Principal's random sample.
\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=6pt]
  \item \ans{Trustworthy}
  \item \ans{Using a random number table — a chance-based method.}
  \item \ans{Yes — every student had an equal chance of being selected.}
  \item \ans{Yes — the random selection makes the conclusion trustworthy for the student population.}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.08in}

\textbf{Scenario C:} Company-sponsored conference doctors.
\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=6pt]
  \item \ans{Not Trustworthy}
  \item \ans{Doctors who attend that company's sponsored conferences — likely favorable toward the company.}
  \item \ans{Doctors who don't attend company events, and doctors who are skeptical of the drug.}
  \item \ans{The estimate of recommendation rates will be inflated; the sample is not representative of all prescribing physicians.}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.08in}

\textbf{Scenario D:} Systematic mail survey.
\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=6pt]
  \item \ans{Yes — selecting every 50th address is a systematic random sample (chance-based).}
  \item \ans{The selection is trustworthy. However, nonresponse bias is a concern: those who respond may have different incomes than those who don't, and the postal database may undercover certain populations (undercoverage).}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.08in}

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\textbf{Group Synthesis:} \ans{A data collection method produces trustworthy conclusions when every individual in the population has a known (ideally equal) chance of being selected, so the sample is likely to represent the population rather than only those with strong opinions or easy access.}
\end{practicebox}
