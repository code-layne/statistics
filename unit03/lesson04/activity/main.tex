\begin{scenariobox}[Context]{sky}
\small
Your group will investigate four flawed surveys. For each survey, identify the type
of bias, explain the mechanism (who is over- or under-represented and why), state the
direction of distortion, and suggest an improvement. Use precise statistical
language --- the AP exam requires you to name the bias AND explain the mechanism.
\end{scenariobox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Survey 1:} A sports website posts an online poll asking visitors: ``Who is
the greatest basketball player of all time?'' By midnight, 15{,}000 votes have
been cast.

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item Type of bias: \blank{7cm}
  \item Who is most likely to respond to this poll? \blank{8cm}
  \item How does this distort the results? (Too many $\ldots$ / Too few $\ldots$)\\
        \writeline\\[1pt]\writeline
  \item Suggest an improvement: \blank{9cm}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Survey 2:} A government agency surveys 500 randomly selected households
about internet usage by mailing a paper survey. Only 180 households return it, and
most returns are from households with elderly residents.

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item The original selection (500 random households): biased? \blank{3cm}
  \item What type of bias affects the final 180 responses? \blank{5cm}
  \item How would this distort the estimate of internet usage? Explain.\\
        \writeline\\[1pt]\writeline
  \item Suggest one way to improve response rates: \blank{9cm}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Survey 3:} A national survey on immigration is conducted using random-digit
dialing to landline phones only. No cell phones are called.

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item Type of bias: \blank{7cm}
  \item What group is under-represented? \blank{8cm}
  \item Why might this group's views on immigration differ from landline owners'?\\
        \writeline
  \item How would this distort the results? \blank{9cm}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\textbf{Survey 4:} A school surveys students: ``In order to improve student safety,
do you support the new ID-badge policy?''

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]
  \item Type of bias: \blank{7cm}
  \item What makes this question biased? \blank{9cm}
  \item Rewrite the question to remove the bias: \blank{9cm}
  \item In which direction would the original question distort the estimate of
        support? \blank{7cm}
\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}
