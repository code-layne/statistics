\begin{scenariobox}[Context]{sky}
\small
A quality inspector tests items from an assembly line one at a time until
finding the first defective item. Each item has a 12\% chance of being
defective, and items are independent. Let $X =$ the number of items
inspected until (and including) the first defective one.
\end{scenariobox}

\vspace{0.1in}

\begin{practicebox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=8pt]

  \item
  B: \ans{Binary --- each item is either defective (success) or non-defective (failure).}

  I: \ans{Independent --- items are independent (given in the problem).}

  T: \ans{Trials until first success --- testing continues until the first defective item.}

  S: \ans{Same $p = 0.12$ for every item.}

  $X \sim \text{Geom}(0.12)$

  \item
  \[
    P(X = 3) = (0.88)^{2} \cdot (0.12) = (0.7744)(0.12) \approx \ans{0.0929}
  \]

  \item $\mu_X = 1/0.12 \approx \ans{8.33}$

  \ans{If the inspector tests items many times until finding the first defective one, the average number of items inspected per defective find would be about 8.33.}

\end{enumerate}
\end{practicebox}
