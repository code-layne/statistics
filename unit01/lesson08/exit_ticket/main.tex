\small A courier company tracked the delivery times (minutes) for 20 orders
on one afternoon. The modified boxplot below summarizes the distribution.

\vspace{0.12in}
\begin{center}
\begin{tikzpicture}[scale=1.0]
  % Axis: 0–70, scale (v)*0.12; ticks every 10
  \draw[thick] (0.3,0) -- (8.8,0);
  \foreach \v/\lbl in {0/0, 1.20/10, 2.40/20, 3.60/30, 4.80/40,
                        6.00/50, 7.20/60, 8.40/70} {
    \draw (\v,-0.12) -- (\v,0.12);
    \node[below, font=\small] at (\v,-0.12) {\lbl};
  }
  \node[below, font=\small\itshape] at (4.2,-0.56) {Delivery time (minutes)};

  % Five-number: min=10 (x=1.20), Q1=18 (x=2.16), M=25 (x=3.00),
  %              Q3=34 (x=4.08), last non-outlier=55 (x=6.60), outlier=60 (x=7.20)
  % scale: v*0.12
  \draw[thick, navy] (1.20, 0.38) -- (2.16, 0.38);   % left whisker
  \draw[thick, navy] (2.16, 0.10) rectangle (4.08, 0.66);  % box Q1→Q3
  \draw[thick, navy] (3.00, 0.10) -- (3.00, 0.66);   % median
  \draw[thick, navy] (4.08, 0.38) -- (6.60, 0.38);   % right whisker to last non-outlier
  \draw[thick, navy] (1.20, 0.22) -- (1.20, 0.54);   % left cap
  \draw[thick, navy] (6.60, 0.22) -- (6.60, 0.54);   % right cap
  \filldraw[navy] (7.20, 0.38) circle (0.11);         % outlier at 60
\end{tikzpicture}
\end{center}
\end{tcolorbox}

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=10pt]

  \item From the boxplot, read the five-number summary and IQR.\\[5pt]
        $\min = \blank{1.5cm}$ \quad $Q_1 = \blank{1.5cm}$ \quad $M = \blank{1.5cm}$
        \quad $Q_3 = \blank{1.5cm}$ \quad last non-outlier $= \blank{1.5cm}$\\[4pt]
        IQR $= Q_3 - Q_1 = \blank{2cm}$ minutes

  \item The outlier dot represents a delivery time of 60 minutes.
        Use the $1.5 \times \text{IQR}$ fence rule to verify that 60 is an outlier.
        Show your work.\\[6pt]
        Upper fence $= Q_3 + 1.5(\text{IQR}) = \blank{7cm}$\\[4pt]
        Is 60 an outlier? \blank{2cm} \quad Because: \blank{6cm}

  \item Approximately what percent of the orders were delivered in 18 to 34
        minutes? \blank{3cm}\%. \quad What does this interval represent?
        \blank{5cm}

  \item The right whisker extends to 55, not 60. Explain what the whisker
        endpoint represents and why the boxplot does not end at 60.\\[6pt]
        \writeline\\[1pt]\writeline

\end{enumerate}
