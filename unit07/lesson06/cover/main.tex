\begin{learningtargetbox}
\small
\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em, itemsep=5pt]
  \item I can identify the two-sample $t$-interval as the appropriate procedure for
        estimating a difference of two population means and describe the shape, center,
        and variability of the sampling distribution of $\bar x_1 - \bar x_2$.
        \textit{(UNC-4.V, Skill 1.D)}
  \item I can verify the conditions required for the two-sample $t$-interval:
        independence and Normal for each sample.
        \textit{(UNC-4.W, Skill 4.C)}
  \item I can compute the standard error and margin of error for the difference of
        two population means.
        \textit{(UNC-4.X, Skill 3.D)}
  \item I can calculate and interpret a two-sample $t$-interval for $\mu_1 - \mu_2$
        in context, noting whether 0 is in the interval.
        \textit{(UNC-4.Y, Skill 3.D)}
\end{enumerate}
\end{learningtargetbox}
