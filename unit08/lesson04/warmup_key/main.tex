A researcher conducts a chi-square goodness-of-fit test to determine whether the
distribution of students across four academic departments matches expected enrollment
proportions. The test produces $\chi^2 = 7.82$ with $df = 3$ and $p$-value $\approx 0.050$.

\begin{enumerate}

  \item At $\alpha = 0.05$, state your decision (reject or fail to reject $H_0$)
        and explain your reasoning in one sentence.

        \textbf{Decision:} \ans{Reject $H_0$}

        \textbf{Reasoning:}
        \ansline{$p = 0.050 \le \alpha = 0.05$, so we reject $H_0$ (borderline --- reject when $p \le \alpha$).}

  \item Write a conclusion in context. Use the form:
        ``We [do / do not] have convincing evidence that\ldots''

        \ansline{We have convincing evidence that the distribution of students across the four departments differs from the expected enrollment proportions.}

  \item What does $df = 3$ tell us about the number of categories in the
        original data? Show your work.

        \ansline{$df = k - 1 \Rightarrow 3 = k - 1 \Rightarrow k = $ \ans{4}. There were 4 categories.}

\end{enumerate}
