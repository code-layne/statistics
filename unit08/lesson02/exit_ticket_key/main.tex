\vspace{0.1in}
A spinner has 5 regions with the following claimed proportions:
A = 0.10, B = 0.20, C = 0.30, D = 0.25, E = 0.15.
The spinner is spun 100 times and the region landed on is recorded each time.

\begin{enumerate}

  \item State H$_0$ and H$_a$ for a chi-square goodness of fit test of whether
        the spinner follows the claimed distribution.

        H$_0$: \ans{$p_A = 0.10$, $p_B = 0.20$, $p_C = 0.30$, $p_D = 0.25$, $p_E = 0.15$
        (the spinner follows the claimed distribution).}

        \vspace{0.05in}

        H$_a$: \ans{At least one of the spinner's true proportions differs from the claimed value.}

        \vspace{0.1in}

  \item Calculate the expected count for each region.

        \begin{center}
        \renewcommand{\arraystretch}{1.4}
        \begin{tabular}{lccc}
            \textbf{Region} & \textbf{Null proportion ($p_0$)} & \textbf{Expected count ($E = 100 \cdot p_0$)} \\
            \hline
            A & 0.10 & \ans{10} \\
            B & 0.20 & \ans{20} \\
            C & 0.30 & \ans{30} \\
            D & 0.25 & \ans{25} \\
            E & 0.15 & \ans{15} \\
        \end{tabular}
        \end{center}

        Are all expected counts greater than 5? \quad \textcolor{keyred}{\textbf{Yes}} \quad (No)

        Justify: \ans{Smallest expected count is $E_A = 10 \ge 5$. All expected counts $\ge 5$,
        so the Large Counts condition is satisfied.}

\end{enumerate}

\begin{teachernote}
    $df = 5 - 1 = 4$. Students do not yet calculate the $\chi^2$ statistic or $p$-value ---
    that is covered in Lesson 8.3. If students finish early, ask them to also state the
    Random condition check for this context.
\end{teachernote}
