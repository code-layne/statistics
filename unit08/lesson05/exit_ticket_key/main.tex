\vspace{0.1in}

For each scenario below: \textbf{(a)} identify the correct chi-square test type and explain
why, and \textbf{(b)} write $H_0$ in words.

\vspace{0.08in}

\begin{enumerate}

  \item A school district randomly selects 80 students from each of 4 grade levels
        (9th, 10th, 11th, 12th) and asks each student which subject (Math, Science, or English)
        is their favorite.

        \textbf{(a)} Test type: \ans{test for homogeneity} \quad Because: \ansline{Four separate random samples (one per grade level) each measure one categorical variable (favorite subject).}

        \textbf{(b)} $H_0$: \ansline{The distribution of favorite subjects (Math/Science/English) is the same across all four grade levels.}

  \item A random sample of 200 adults is asked their marital status
        (Married, Single, or Other) and whether they exercise regularly (Yes or No).

        \textbf{(a)} Test type: \ans{test for independence} \quad Because: \ansline{One random sample measures two categorical variables (marital status and exercise habit) on each individual.}

        \textbf{(b)} $H_0$: \ansline{There is no association between marital status and exercise habits in the adult population.}

\end{enumerate}
