\begin{notesbox}{Spiral Review --- Test Type and Hypotheses (Lessons 8.4--8.5)}

For each scenario, \textbf{identify the type of chi-square test} (homogeneity or independence)
and \textbf{write $H_0$}.

\bigskip

\textbf{1.} A pediatrician randomly selects 50 children from each of 4 age groups
(2--4, 5--7, 8--10, and 11--13 years) and records which vaccine they received first
(Vaccine A, B, or C).

\vspace{0.08in}
Test type (circle one): \quad \ans{Homogeneity} \quad (4 separate groups; one variable measured)

\vspace{0.08in}
$H_0$: \ansline{The distribution of first vaccine received is the same across all four age groups.}

\vspace{0.15in}

\textbf{2.} A random sample of 200 adults is asked their income level
(Low / Medium / High) and their reported happiness level (Low / Medium / High).

\vspace{0.08in}
Test type (circle one): \quad \ans{Independence} \quad (one sample; two categorical variables)

\vspace{0.08in}
$H_0$: \ansline{There is no association between income level and happiness level in the adult population (the variables are independent).}

\end{notesbox}
