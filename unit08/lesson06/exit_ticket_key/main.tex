A researcher conducts a chi-square test for independence on a random sample of US adults to
determine whether \textbf{political affiliation} (Democrat / Independent / Republican) and
\textbf{preferred news source} (Online / Television / Print) are associated.
The test gives $\chi^2 = 8.45$, $\text{df} = 4$, p-value $\approx 0.077$. Use $\alpha = 0.05$.

\begin{enumerate}[itemsep=14pt]

  \item \textbf{Interpret the p-value} in context.

        \ansline{The probability of obtaining a chi-square statistic of 8.45 or larger, given that political affiliation and preferred news source are independent among US adults, is approximately 0.077.}
        \ansline{}
        \ansline{}

  \item \textbf{State a complete conclusion} at $\alpha = 0.05$.

        \ansline{Because $p \approx 0.077 > \alpha = 0.05$, we fail to reject $H_0$. We do not have convincing evidence of an association between political affiliation and preferred news source among US adults.}
        \ansline{}
        \ansline{}

\end{enumerate}
