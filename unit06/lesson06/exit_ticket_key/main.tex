$H_0: p = 0.20$; $H_a: p \ne 0.20$; $z\approx1.50$; $p$-value $\approx 0.134$; $\alpha=0.05$.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Hypotheses stated.

  \item Because $p$-value $=$ \ans{0.134} \ans{$>$} $\alpha =$ \ans{0.05}, we \ans{fail to reject} $H_0$.

  \item \ansline{We do not have sufficient evidence that the true proportion of millennials who buy physical newspapers differs from 20\%.}

  \item \ansline{Yes --- at $\alpha=0.10$, $p$-value $=0.134 > 0.10$ still, so we still fail to reject $H_0$. No change in decision at $\alpha=0.10$.}
\end{enumerate}
