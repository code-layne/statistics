A health researcher collected independent random samples from two age groups to compare the
proportion who reported getting at least 7 hours of sleep per night.
Group 1 (adults aged 18--34): $n_1 = 200$, $\hat p_1 = 0.54$.
Group 2 (adults aged 35--54): $n_2 = 180$, $\hat p_2 = 0.46$.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Point estimate: $\hat p_1 - \hat p_2 = $ \ans{$0.54 - 0.46 = 0.08$}

  \item $SE = \sqrt{0.54(0.46)/200 + 0.46(0.54)/180} = \sqrt{0.001242 + 0.001380} = \sqrt{0.002622} \approx \ans{0.0512}$

  \item Interval: $(\ans{-0.021}, \ans{0.181})$

  \item Contains 0? \ans{Yes} \quad \ansline{Since 0 is in the interval, there is no convincing evidence of a difference between the proportions of 18--34 year olds and 35--54 year olds who get at least 7 hours of sleep per night.}
\end{enumerate}
