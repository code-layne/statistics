A health researcher collected independent random samples from two age groups to compare the
proportion who reported getting at least 7 hours of sleep per night.
Group 1 (adults aged 18--34): $n_1 = 200$, $\hat p_1 = 0.54$.
Group 2 (adults aged 35--54): $n_2 = 180$, $\hat p_2 = 0.46$.

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Compute the point estimate for $p_1 - p_2$. \blank{4cm}

  \item The researcher constructs a 95\% two-sample $z$-interval.
        Compute $SE_{\hat p_1 - \hat p_2}$.
        \writelines{2}

  \item The interval is $0.08 \pm 1.960(0.0518) = 0.08 \pm 0.101$.
        Write the interval: $(\blank{2.5cm}, \blank{2.5cm})$.

  \item Does this interval contain 0? \blank{2cm} \quad
        What does that tell us about $p_1$ and $p_2$? \writeline
\end{enumerate}
